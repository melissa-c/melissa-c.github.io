Motivation

C7: 8th Feb 2016

Search Inside Yourself: Chapter 6

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

To pursue a life with a higher purpose and passion sustains happiness much longer than pursuing happiness through temporary pleasures such as external rewards. This is because passion and a higher purpose requires you to be more engaged with acquiring happiness and will have more meaning to you - especially if the happiness is derived from something altruistic or larger than ourselves.
As I get older, the pursuit of happiness through finding a higher purpose is playing a larger role. It can be difficult when you don’t feel like you know what you want or when your financial, geographical, etc situation may prevent or delay these pursuits.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise:

What are your core values?

Compassion: being empathetic and behaving in a kind and respectful way to living things and the environment.
Justice: upholding and fighting for equality and fairness.
Truth: seeking it out and being held to account if it wanes.
Creativity: injecting a bit of fun, humour, and a different perspective on things. Giving life to things that didn’t exist before. Transforming ideas into reality. Letting the inner child play.

What do you stand for?

Human and animal rights: supporting and speaking out for civil rights; gender equality; the humane treatment of animals; the right to live in a safe environment and have access to education, sanitation, clean water and food, healthcare, etc.
Sustainability: supporting, encouraging, and living in an environmentally-conscious way.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

The resilience ideas make sense and seem like a logical way to overcome challenges. It’s helpful having these mental tools for handling emotions and failures in a more objective way. The meditation exercise was useful and made me reflect on how I personalise failure and can get frustrated. Time, hindsight, and analysis can put those feelings into perspective but I need more practise in resilience and mindfulness for when the events are happening in real time.

Any other musings?

The story about Roz Savage writing two versions of her own obituary hit home for me. The courage she had to pursue something that I would find terrifying was also really inspirational.
