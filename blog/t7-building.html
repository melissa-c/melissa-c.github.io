Building

T7: 18th Feb 2016

Did you learn anything while building the calculator?

Aside from never taking my calculator or other machinery for granted again...

  • Order and scope is important: Trying to logically think through and anticipate as many possible situations, outcomes, etc and trying to deal with each one in the correct order to make it all work. I couldn't quite make the calculator handle oddballs like handling negative numbers because there'd be double operators, or not allowing multiple decimals to be entered for one number.
  • Vastness of JS: My current lack of the correct vocabulary and knowledge of what JS is capable of was very apparent when tackling some of the issues. But learning workarounds is always beneficial too.
  • Other things: Along the way when researching I found some other interesting tidbits but I didn't use them for my calculator, such as the short version of the if loop:
    condition ? expression1 : expression2

What was your experience extending the racer game?

I struggled with solving how to move from one row to the next. Still can't get it to work, but will go back to it if there's time. It was interesting tidying up the old file. It was relatively well ordered so it wasn't too difficult to build on to it.
It's always difficult too, to leave something unfinished. But I'm slowly learning to accept just moving on sometimes and try to make it as function as possible, then go back if there's time.
