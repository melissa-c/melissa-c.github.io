Meditation and Process

C3: 4th Jan 2016

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

It made me think more about the structure of the website to try to better understand the "bones" of the site and try to set it up in a hopefully logical and decipherable way.

I found it difficult not to spend too much time on certain things, not necessarily to make things look perfect, but because I wanted to understand why something wasn't working.

Search Inside Yourself: Chapter 2

What did you think about meditation before reading the chapter?

I never really considered meditation much prior to reading chapter 2. Or, rather, I never labelled it as such. I had thought about the importance and positive effects of clearing your mind, relaxation, having time to yourself, etc for general well-being and when tackling stressful situations or tasks.

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

Meta-attention was interesting as I hadn't considered it separately from attention before. Also, thinking about training the brain like going to the gym and the scientific studies on the affect of meditation to the brain.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

I liked the idea of starting small with intention and the general posture and breathing. I'm not such a fan of the mantra-styled meditations. I also need to practice more of the letting go of distractions, etc.

Any other musings?

It was intriguing thinking about the mind having a default state of happiness. Though I guess it would be the ideal or goal of having happiness as a default rather than the reality for people dealing with mental illness or other factors.
