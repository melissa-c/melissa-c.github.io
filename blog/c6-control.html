Control

C6: 8th Feb 2016

Search Inside Yourself: Chapter 5

"One can have no smaller or greater mastery than mastery of oneself"
- Leonardo da Vinci

What learnings did you take from the chapter?

Understanding yourself is important in having more control over your emotions and ability to deal with challenging situations as it enables the power of choice. I found the insight that self-regulation is not about suppressing emotions to be a relevant reminder to be more skilful in my handling of my own emotions.
The idea in some meditative traditions that the mind is a sense was an interesting point of view I’d never considered.
I’m not sure I fully understand or agree with the idea that letting go can potentially eliminate pain, including physical pain. While I can certainly see some truths and clear benefits of letting go - particularly from a psychological standpoint, the extent of the benefits seem a bit of a stretch to me - at least at this stage of my mindfulness learnings.

How did you find the two minutes of mindfulness?

For the mindfulness exercise I chose to do the Siberian North Railroad trigger handling exercise as I felt it would be beneficial. It was helpful to go through an emotional event from a more objective point of view and also have the benefits of hindsight and (hopefully) a more mature thought process. Deconstructing a past experience and analysing how I could have handled it better is humbling and an important lesson that I can hopefully build on and apply in the future.

When you were pair programming, did you exercise any of your emotional intelligence skills?

I’m yet to pair program but will keep in mind the emotional intelligence skills when the time comes.
